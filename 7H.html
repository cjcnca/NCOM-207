The 7 Habits

For First-Time Job Seekers

Stephen Covey Applied

1. Be Proactive

Take Initiative

Don't wait for a job. Find opportunities, follow up on apps, and ask for accommodations.

Choose Action

Take responsibility for your choices instead of blaming others.

2. Begin with the End in Mind

Have a Vision

Know what kind of career path you want.

Tailor your resume to align with that specific goal.

Career Roadmap

3. Put First Things First

High Priority Task Low Priority (Avoid)
Resume Preparation Social Media Browsing
Networking & Skills Procrastination

4. Think Win-Win

Collaboration

Mutual Benefit

In interviews, show how you help the company.

Your skills help them, and they help you grow.

5. Seek First to Understand

Listen First

Listen carefully to interview questions before you answer.

Ask Why

Understand your manager's goals before starting a project.

6. Synergize

Team Synergy

Value teamwork and diverse strengths.

Show employers you respect different perspectives.

Working Together

7. Sharpen the Saw

  • Constant Learning: Update your skills often.
  • Health & Balance: Take care of your mental health.
  • Renewal: Rest is vital for long-term success.

In-Class Activity

"The Interview Dilemma"

Scenario: You are in an interview. The manager is confused about how to work with an interpreter.

Task: Which 2 habits will you use to turn this into a "Win-Win"?
